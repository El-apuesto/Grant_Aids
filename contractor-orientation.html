Contractor Orientation

The Problem We Solve

Small rural towns across the country need things like:

  • New water systems and infrastructure
  • Road improvements and bridges
  • Fire trucks and emergency equipment
  • Community clinics and health services
  • Parks, playgrounds, and community centers

But these tiny communities don't have the staff or expertise to find grants and write competitive applications. That's where we come in.

Our Business Model

We act as the outsourced grant office for communities that can't afford their own planner or grant writer.

What We Do:

  • Find opportunities: Research and identify grants that match client needs
  • Plan projects: Help communities define and scope their projects properly
  • Write applications: Prepare competitive grant applications
  • Manage deadlines: Keep track of multiple applications and requirements
  • Post-award support: Sometimes help with reporting after they win

We're essentially a part-time grant department that serves multiple small clients who couldn't justify a full-time position.

Who Our Clients Are

Our typical clients include:

  • Tiny rural towns (populations under 5,000)
  • Rural water districts serving small communities
  • Volunteer fire departments in rural areas
  • Small rural nonprofits with limited capacity

Key characteristic: These clients have very limited staff, tight budgets, and almost no time. They need someone who can work independently and communicate clearly when they need input.

Your Role as a Contractor

As part of our contractor team, you might work on:

  • Grant research: Finding funding opportunities that match client needs
  • Application writing: Drafting sections or complete grant proposals
  • Editing and review: Polishing applications before submission
  • Information gathering: Working with clients to collect needed details
  • Organization: Tracking deadlines, requirements, and deliverables
  • Progress reporting: Helping clients report on funded projects

We match tasks to your strengths. Some people are natural researchers, others excel at writing, and some are organizational wizards. All roles are valuable.

How We Work

Work Style:

  • Remote/online: Work from anywhere with reliable internet
  • Checklist-driven: Clear tasks with specific deadlines
  • Collaborative: Regular check-ins and communication
  • Flexible: Workload varies with grant cycles

Our Expectations:

  • Communicate early if you're stuck or confused
  • Meet deadlines (grant deadlines are non-negotiable)
  • Prioritize quality and accuracy over speed
  • Be honest about your capacity and availability

We value contractors who ask questions, flag potential issues early, and take pride in their work.

Immediate Plans (Next 3-6 Months)

Here's what we're working on right now and in the near term:

Current Focus: Building our client base with 3-5 rural communities who need ongoing grant support.
January-February 2026: Active grant writing season for spring/summer funding cycles. Multiple applications in progress.
February-March 2026: Developing templates and systems to work more efficiently as we scale.
Spring 2026: Expanding contractor team as workload grows with new clients.

Right now we're in a growth phase - building relationships with communities, writing active grants, and refining our processes.

Training and Support

We understand that grant writing is specialized work. Here's how we support you:

  • Initial orientation: Overview of our processes and expectations
  • Grant-specific training: Background on each funding program you work with
  • Templates and examples: Access to successful past applications
  • Regular feedback: Constructive review of your work
  • Available for questions: Quick response when you need clarification

Grant experience is helpful but not always required. If you're a strong writer who can follow detailed instructions and research effectively, we can teach you the grant-specific aspects. The right attitude and work ethic matter more than prior experience for many tasks.

Why This Work Matters

This isn't just paperwork - it's helping communities that are often overlooked and underserved.

When we help a town of 800 people win a $500,000 water system grant, we're helping families get safe drinking water. When we secure a $100,000 fire truck grant for a rural department, we're improving emergency response times that could save lives.

These small communities face unique challenges:

  • Shrinking tax bases as young people move to cities
  • Aging infrastructure that needs replacement
  • Limited staff who wear many hats
  • Difficulty competing for grants against larger municipalities

We level the playing field by bringing professional grant expertise to communities that need it most.
