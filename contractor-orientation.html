Contractor Orientation

Welcome!

We help small rural towns win grant money for things like water systems, roads, fire trucks, clinics, and parks. This short tour shows how you might work with us as a contractor.

This will take about 5 minutes to walk through. Let's get started!

What We Do

We act as the part-time grant office for small towns that don't have their own planner or grant writer. We find grants, plan projects, write applications, and sometimes help after they win.

Think of us as the grant department these communities can't afford to hire full-time. We bridge the gap between their needs and available funding opportunities.

Who We Serve

Most clients are tiny towns, rural water or fire districts, and small nonprofits in rural areas. They usually have very limited staff and time.

These communities often have big needs but small budgets. That's where grant funding makes a real difference—replacing aging infrastructure, improving public safety, and expanding community services.

Types of Work You Might Do

As a contractor, you might help with:

  • Researching grant opportunities that match client needs
  • Writing or editing grant applications
  • Organizing project details and tracking deadlines
  • Gathering information from clients
  • Helping with simple progress reports

Different projects need different skills, and we try to match tasks to people's strengths. Grant experience is helpful but not required for some roles—we can provide training for the right person.

What to Expect

Working with us means:

  • Mostly online work: You can work from anywhere with reliable internet
  • Checklists and deadlines: We stay organized with clear tasks and due dates
  • Early communication: If something is confusing, speak up right away—we want to help
  • Quality over speed: We value honesty and thorough work more than rushing

We understand that contractors balance multiple commitments. Clear communication about your availability helps everyone succeed.

Availability & Requirements

Workload: Variable depending on grant cycles. Some weeks may be light, others may have urgent deadlines when applications are due. We try to give as much advance notice as possible.

Basic requirements:

  • Reliable internet connection
  • Basic computer skills (email, word processing, spreadsheets)
  • Ability to read and follow written instructions
  • Comfortable working independently

You'll be working as an independent contractor, not a full-time employee. This means flexible scheduling but also self-directed work.

Our Values

We care about helping small communities that are often overlooked. We try to be patient, respectful, and practical with every client.

Rural communities face unique challenges—limited resources, shrinking populations, aging infrastructure. When we help them secure funding, we're helping improve real lives: better water quality, safer roads, equipped fire departments, and accessible healthcare.

If that mission resonates with you, you might be a great fit for our team.
