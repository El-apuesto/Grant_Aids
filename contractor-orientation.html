Contractor Orientation

Welcome!

We help small rural towns win grant money for things like water systems, roads, fire trucks, clinics, and parks. This short tour shows how you might work with us as a contractor.

This will take about 5 minutes. Let's get started!

What We Do

We act as the part-time grant office for small towns that don't have their own planner or grant writer. We find grants, plan projects, write applications, and sometimes help after they win.

Think of us as the grant department these communities can't afford to hire full-time.

Who We Serve

Most clients are tiny towns, rural water or fire districts, and small nonprofits in rural areas. They usually have very limited staff and time.

These communities often have big needs but small budgets. That's where grant funding makes a real difference.

Types of Work You Might Do

As a contractor, you might help with:

  • Researching grant opportunities
  • Writing or editing grant applications
  • Organizing details and deadlines
  • Gathering information from clients
  • Helping with simple progress reports

Different projects need different skills, and we try to match tasks to people's strengths.

What to Expect

Working with us means:

  • Mostly online work: You can work from anywhere with reliable internet
  • Checklists and deadlines: We stay organized with clear tasks and due dates
  • Early communication: If something is confusing, speak up right away
  • Quality over speed: We value honesty and thorough work more than rushing

Availability & Requirements

Workload: Variable depending on grant cycles. Some weeks may be light, others may have urgent deadlines.

Basic requirements:

  • Reliable internet connection
  • Basic computer skills
  • Ability to read and follow written instructions

Grant experience is helpful but not required for some roles. We can provide training for the right person.

Our Values

We care about helping small communities that are often overlooked. We try to be patient, respectful, and practical with every client.

Rural communities face unique challenges. When we help them secure funding, we're helping improve real lives—better water quality, safer roads, equipped fire departments, and accessible healthcare.
